In early 2025, I performed a manual 100 Bevacizumab article PRISMA study, and introduced each paper separately into an LLM to obtain summaries. Many outputs contained trial information, which were all combined and analyzed using a reasoning model - yielding new insights regarding the cancer drug across several indications at scale. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.14968162

When OpenAI released deep research that year, I published several additional trial related studies, the most challenging being a June 2025 work with 40 Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma (PDAC) meta-analyses totaling 400,000 words, many containing clinical trial articles. Gemini scored a Daraxonrasib drug combination as the top candidate for my startup to receive funding. The Gemini findings then served as inputs for OpenAI and Anthropic to generate Daraxonrasib-based digital twin proposals. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.15735068

This year, I added the Physical AI component to Daraxonrasib-based oncology trial studies using Claude Code. This process required adapting current FDA, ICH, and NIH regulations and templates to a new state of the art AI standard. Resulting documents were then used to draft new legislation, Phase 1 and Phase 2 trial protocols, an Investigational New Drug application, and a recent NIH “Daraxonrasib Phase 1 LLM-Directed Robotic Whipple in KRAS-Mutated PDAC” trial funding application. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21232965
